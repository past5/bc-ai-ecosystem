What We Found

In May 2025, we surveyed members of British Columbia's artificial intelligence community to understand their experiences, challenges, and aspirations. The results paint a picture of an ecosystem with significant strengths alongside persistent barriers.

Primary Finding: While most respondents report positive community experiences, systemic challenges around funding, coordination, and access create barriers that prevent the ecosystem from reaching its full potential.

The survey captured responses from 41 individuals across various sectors, including researchers, entrepreneurs, developers, and other AI practitioners. Despite the relatively small sample size, clear patterns emerged that warrant attention from community leaders and policymakers.

Critical Issues Identified

Three Major Challenges

1. Funding Gap: With 97 funding-related mentions, access to capital emerged as the most significant barrier to ecosystem growth.

2. Coordination Problems: 19 mentions of fragmentation indicate that existing efforts lack coordination, potentially duplicating work and missing synergies.

3. Government Disconnect: Only 32 mentions of government or policy issues suggest weak connections between the AI community and public sector decision-makers.

The Real Story

In May 2025, 41 people completed a quick survey about the BC AI community. Among this small, self‑selected group, most reported feeling welcomed, suggesting cautious optimism rather than broad exclusion.

Key Finding: 71 % of respondents feel welcomed in BC’s AI spaces within this sample. Larger, representative research is needed before drawing conclusions about the whole ecosystem.

Challenges remain, but the data hints at existing strengths that could be scaled—provided specific barriers are addressed.

Evidence‑Informed Recommendations

Priority moves based on what the 41 respondents told us:

  • Address Financial Barriers: Create affordable entry points and support for newcomers.
  • Scale Proven Community Tactics: Build on methods already making 71 % feel welcome.
  • Embed Accountability: Formalize oversight and governance to meet the top ethics concern.
  • Sustain Human Connection: Design AI initiatives that enhance—not replace—human relationships.
  • Keep Engagement Consistent: Help people stay connected over time to prevent alienation.
  • Focus on Practical Ethics: Tackle monetization, bias, and transparency head‑on.

A Balanced Perspective

Within this exploratory snapshot, BC’s AI community appears largely welcoming, yet specific barriers—especially cost—remain.

The next step is to validate these findings with a larger, representative survey, including demographic data to pinpoint which groups still feel excluded.

BC’s AI ecosystem shows promise. Let’s make sure it’s truly open to all.
